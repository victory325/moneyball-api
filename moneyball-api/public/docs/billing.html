Before submitting your deposit request, check the following in order to ensure a successful deposit:

1. Did you enter the Primary Email Address used with your PayPal account?

The PayPal Email Address you enter on this form must always match the Primary Email Address on your PayPal account. To check primary PayPal email address, log in to your PayPal account and go to www.paypal.com/PROFILE.

2. Do you have a confirmed Address on your PayPal account?

$BALL only accepts deposits from PayPal accounts that have a confirmed address. PayPal confirms an address when you enter a valid credit card on your PayPal account. To modify or confirm your address with PayPal, log in to your PayPal account and go to www.paypal.com/PROFILE.

3. Have you already signed up another $BALL user account using PayPal address?

Only one PayPal account is allowed per account username. Your deposit will not be accepted if your PayPal email address is already being used by another $BALL account to make deposit transactions.

4. State Permitting?

Certain U.S. states do not currently support cash games. If your PayPal address is from one of the following restricted states, $BALL will not be able to accept your deposit request. Restricted states include: Arkansas, Connecticut, Delaware, Illinois, Louisiana, Maryland, Montana, South Carolina, South Dakota, and Tennessee.

Per the rules and regulations defined by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) as it relates to online/mobile skill cash tournaments, $BALL is required to report winnings to the IRS as "Other Income" when a U.S. player reaches/exceeds $600 in Net Winnings at the end of the calendar year.

If you have reached/exceeded $600 in Net Winnings at the end of the year, then you will be sent a 1099-Misc form and will be required to declare your Net Winnings when filing a tax return.

What are Net Winnings?

The IRS requires $BALL to calculate a player’s “Net Winnings” as the total winnings minus the entry fee’s paid in tournaments where the player has not won a cash prize. Net winnings will continue to increase each time a player wins or receives cash winnings of some sort. This calculation is based solely on user net winnings, not the amount of withdraw transactions made over the course of a calendar year.

Net winnings are calculated as your total winnings minus the entry fees for the tournaments that you've won. Entry fees for cash games in which a cash prize wasn't awarded are not calculated in the player's net winnings.

Example: If you played five tournaments with an entry fee of $5 and won three of them for $20 each, your net winnings would be $45. That is, your total winnings ($60 or three times $20) minus the entry fees for the games won ($15 or three times $5).
